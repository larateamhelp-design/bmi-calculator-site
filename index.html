BMI categories for adults

BMI is a quick screening measurement. It is useful for population-level health guidance, but it does not replace medical advice or measure body composition directly.

Underweight

BMI below 18.5. A clinician can help evaluate nutrition, medical causes, or fitness goals.

Healthy weight

BMI from 18.5 to 24.9. This is the standard healthy BMI range for most adults.

Overweight or obesity

BMI 25 or higher. BMI is only one signal, so consider waist size, blood pressure, labs, and activity level too.

BMI calculator for women by age

Adult BMI categories are the same for women and men, but age, pregnancy, menopause, muscle mass, and body fat distribution can affect interpretation.

When BMI is less useful

BMI may be misleading for athletes, pregnant people, older adults with muscle loss, and anyone with unusual body composition.

BMI calculator FAQ

How do I calculate BMI using kg and cm?

Convert centimeters to meters, then divide kilograms by meters squared. Example: 68 kg and 170 cm is 68 / 1.70² = 23.5.

How do I calculate BMI using pounds and feet?

Convert your height to total inches, divide pounds by inches squared, then multiply by 703.

Is BMI different for women and men?

The adult formula is the same. The interpretation may differ depending on body composition, pregnancy, age, and health history.

Does age affect BMI results?

For adults 20+, standard BMI categories are commonly used. For children and teens, BMI should be interpreted with age- and sex-specific percentiles.

What is a healthy BMI range?

For most adults, 18.5 to 24.9 is considered healthy, 25 to 29.9 is overweight, and 30 or above is obesity.
